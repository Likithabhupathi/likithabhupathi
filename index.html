Sterilization Methods in Microbiology

An educational guide for microbiology students

Physical Sterilization Methods

Autoclave (Moist Heat Sterilization)

Autoclaving is the most commonly used sterilization method in microbiology laboratories. It uses saturated steam under pressure to kill microorganisms.

  • Temperature: 121°C
  • Pressure: 15 psi
  • Time: 15–20 minutes
  • Uses: Culture media, glassware, surgical instruments

Dry Heat Sterilization

Dry heat sterilization is carried out using a hot air oven. It kills microorganisms by oxidative damage to cellular components.

  • Temperature: 160–180°C
  • Time: 1–2 hours
  • Uses: Glassware, metal instruments, powders

Filtration

Filtration removes microorganisms from heat-sensitive liquids using membrane filters. It does not kill organisms but physically removes them.

  • Common pore size: 0.22 μm
  • Uses: Vaccines, enzymes, antibiotics

Chemical Sterilization Methods

Alcohols and Aldehydes

Alcohols and aldehydes are widely used chemical disinfectants. They act by denaturing proteins and disrupting cell membranes.

  • Examples: Ethanol, Isopropanol, Formaldehyde
  • Uses: Surface disinfection, skin antisepsis

Ethylene Oxide (Gas Sterilization)

Ethylene oxide is a gaseous sterilant used for heat-sensitive and moisture-sensitive medical equipment.

  • Advantages: Deep penetration
  • Uses: Plastic syringes, catheters, medical devices
